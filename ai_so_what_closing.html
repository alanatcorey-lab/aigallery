So,
what now?

Myths have been busted. Best practices have been named. A real organization's two-year journey has been mapped. The question this gallery leaves you with is not "what is AI?" It is "what will you do differently on Monday?"

Three questions to sit with

01

Where does your organization sit on the AI readiness spectrum?

Not in terms of tools, but in terms of culture, psychological safety, and leadership posture. Are you waiting for a strategy, or already experimenting?

02

What is the one workflow on your team where the human-AI handoff is unclear?

Not the easy ones. The ones where accountability feels murky, where AI outputs are being used but no one has agreed on who reviews them or what happens when they're wrong.

03

What would it take for your team to feel safe enough to say "the AI got this wrong"?

Psychological safety around AI errors is not automatic. It has to be built, modeled by leadership, and protected through norms. What is one thing you could do this week to create that space?

Before you leave

Name one thing your team could do this week to become more AI-ready.

Not a policy. Not a tool purchase. Not a training program. One small, human thing, a conversation, a norm, a question worth asking out loud, that moves your team one step closer to the culture that makes AI work.
